---
title: Higher-Kinded Types 
---

_Part of a series of learnings from <http://lambdaconf.us/>_

A higher-kinded type takes a type (or multiple types) in order to
be fully defined. It's like currying or partial application.

For example, tuples:
`tuple<number, number> => point`
